Legacies

Leaving us a legacy in your will is another way that you can help. To ensure that this legacy is spent locally you need to specify that it should go to the Cambridge & District RSPCA Branch, registered charity number 205098. This charity number is unique, so is the best way of identifying us as a beneficiary.

Legacies to RSPCA branches are normally handled via the Legacy department of the national RSPCA, who have the expertise to deal with issues involved in handling the administration of an estate. If you direct your executors to forward your legacy to them at RSPCA, Wilberforce Way, Southwater, Horsham, Sussex RH13 9RS, they will arrange for the funds to be transferred to the Cambridge & District branch.

If you wish the RSPCA to act as your executor and administer your estate, this can also be arranged. Please look through the national website details about naming the RSPCA as your executor.

House Clearance

RSPCA Cambridge & District volunteers will do house clearances at no charge, subject to there being a reasonable proportion of saleable items in the contents removed. These will then be used to raise funds for our local animal welfare work.

Rubbish and unsaleable items are disposed of via licensed contractors (usually the local authority trade waste for which we pay a fee). As we are responsible for the health and well-being of our volunteers we cannot take hazardous materials (eg asbestos sheeting), but we can remove and dispose of normal, non-hazardous rubbish (card, old newspapers etc.)

We can visit premises anywhere in the general area of Cambridge at no charge to check whether it would be financially viable for our volunteers to clear it. As a general rule we wouldn't be able to clear somewhere if there was large-scale contamination of any kind (eg fire or water damage, rotting or rodent-infested waste).

To discuss getting a house or flat cleared, please email treasurer@rspca-cambridge.org.uk, including a contact phone number where you can be reached at evenings or the weekend so that we can call you back to discuss it.

Please note: We need a minimum of 7 days notice to do a house clearance because it takes a while to notify the volunteers and fix a time when enough of them are available.

We can sell:

  • Books (old and new)
  • Clothing and Linens (undamaged items can be sold in our shop; worn or damaged ones are sold in bulk for recycling)
  • Records, tapes, CDs, videos, DVDs
  • Household items such as china, ornaments, pictures, cookware
  • Furniture (larger items will be sold on to a local dealer as our shop space is limited)
  • Foreign coins, medals, stamps - in fact anything "collectable"
  • Electrical goods in working condition (will be sold on to a dealer who can do the necessary safety checks)
  • Rugs and carpets (if in very good condition)
  • House plants
  • Toys and games
